Overview

Synopsis

UNMASKED is a feature-length documentary exploring human sensitivity toward facial perception.

Michel's Present-Day Struggle

A deeply personal journey as he seeks employment.

The Ghost of Joseph Merrick

Historical parallels of Michel's experience and Merrick's abandonment in Brussels in 1886.

Expert & Celebrity Voices

Interviews with philosophers, reconstructive surgeons, celebrities and activists.

Visual Style

A blend of intimate vérité, stylized reenactments (Merrick's past), and essay-film interludes.

Key Themes & Thematic Structure

The documentary is structured thematically, with each section exploring a different dimension of the central issue. The key themes include:

Face & Identity

The significance of facial perception in human interaction

Stigma & Prejudice

The social biases faced by individuals with visible differences

Work & Inclusion

Michel's personal journey to find employment

Medical & Scientific Advances

Insights from reconstructive and cancer biology experts

History & Reflection

The case of Joseph Merrick and its relevance today

Why this film?

A Story That Has Never Been More Relevant

Disability & Employment

1 in 5 Europeans has a disability, yet unemployment rates are 3x higher than average (Eurostat).

Brussels' Dark History

Merrick's abandonment in Belgium adds a haunting layer—how far have we really come?

A Universal Theme

"How do we treat those who look 'other'?"

Unique Approach - Unlike medical-focused disability docs, UNMASKED frames Michel's job hunt as an existential quest for dignity

Treatment & Story Approach

The documentary will follow a thematic approach rather than a strictly chronological narrative.

There will be no voiceover narration; the story will unfold through:

Michel's day-to-day experiences

focusing on his search for employment and interactions with society.

Intimate conversations with Michel's brother

revealing personal and familial perspectives

Medical and scientific insights

including interviews with experts from VIB-KU Leuven Center for Cancer Biology.

Philosophical and sociological perspectives

including discussions with philosophers.

Reflections from public figures

who spoke about living with facial differences.

Historical reference to Joseph Merrick's

abandonment in Brussels connections between past and present.

The approach will be immersive and observational, allowing the audience to experience Michel's world without artificial dramatization.

The goal is to create an emotional yet intellectually engaging film that challenges perceptions.

Director's Statement

UNMASKED is a personal exploration of perception and identity. Rather than framing Michel as an object of sympathy, the film emphasizes his agency, resilience, and humanity. By immersing audiences in Michel's world, the film challenges how we judge others based on appearance.

I am deeply committed to telling this story with sensitivity, authenticity, and respect. My goal is to create a film that not only resonates with audiences but also inspires them to see the world—and each other—with greater empathy and understanding.

Visual Style and Approach

The visual style of "Unmasked" will be intimate and cinematic

Cinematic

naturalistic cinematography emphasizing intimacy

Observational

storytelling avoiding artificial dramatization

Interviews

shot in dynamic, engaging settings

Minimalist

immersive sound design


The film will have a poetic, emotional tone, using music and sound design to enhance the storytelling.

Conclusion

"Unmasked" is an intimate exploration of identity, self-acceptance, and resilience. Through Michel's personal journey, the film aims to challenge conventional ideas of beauty, success, and human worth.

The documentary is both a personal narrative and a social commentary on how appearance affects the way we are treated, and ultimately, the way we see ourselves.

By sharing Michel's story, we hope to ignite a broader conversation about the need for a more inclusive and compassionate world—one where worth is defined by who we are on the inside, not just how we look on the outside.
